The Founding Story

Mongolian heritage.
British design.
No compromise.

Meg Genden didn't start Oxford Meadow to build a brand. She started it because she couldn't find a single piece of cashmere she trusted completely — not the sourcing, not the wages paid to the people who made it, not the claim that it would last.

Her answer was to go directly to Mongolia. To sit with herder families in Övörkhangai. To understand the land and the animal and the craft before designing a single garment. To bridge centuries-old Mongolian craftsmanship with a modern, minimal form that would never go out of style.

"What you wear should care for you — not just adorn you. And it should care for the people who made it."
— Meg Genden, Founder

Supply Chain

From Herder to You

Every stage of your garment's journey is documented, audited, and published. Hover each stage to learn more.

01
Herder Families
340+ partner families. Hand-combed, never sheared. Goats roam 2.3M hectares of GCS-certified pastureland. Paid above market rate, always.
02
Fibre Grading
Independent third-party grading. Only fibres under 15.5 microns pass. The industry average is 19µ. We reject the rest, every time.
03
Ulaanbaatar Atelier
Knitted and hand-finished in our partner atelier in Ulaanbaatar. Fair wages. Safe conditions. Annual independent audits — results public.
04
Carbon-Neutral to You
Recycled packaging. Zero virgin plastic. Fully offset last-mile delivery. Your garment arrives with a QR code linking to its full provenance report.
