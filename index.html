The Founding Story

Mongolian heritage.
British design.
No compromise.

Meg Genden didn't start Oxford Meadow to build a brand. She started it because she couldn't find a single piece of cashmere she trusted completely — not the sourcing, not the wages, not the claim it would last.

Her answer was to go directly to Mongolia. To sit with herder families in Övörkhangai. To understand the land and the animal before designing a single garment. To bridge centuries-old Mongolian craftsmanship with a modern, minimal form that would never go out of style.

"What you wear should care for you — not just adorn you."
— Meg Genden, Founder

Supply Chain

From Herder to You

Hover each stage to see how your garment travels from the Mongolian steppe to your wardrobe — with full traceability at every step.

01
Herder Families
340+ partner families. Hand-combed, never sheared. Goats roam 2.3M hectares of certified pastureland. Paid above market, always.
02
Fibre Grading
Independent third-party grading. Only fibres under 15.5µm pass. The industry allows 19µm. We reject the rest, every time.
03
Ulaanbaatar Atelier
Knitted and hand-finished in our partner atelier. Fair wages. Annual independent audits — results published.
04
Carbon-Neutral to You
Recycled packaging, zero virgin plastic, fully offset delivery. QR code inside links to your garment's full provenance report.
